Who Am I?

I'm Sam Hildebrand, a 17 year old amateur musician and music lover. My background and interest in music stems from the old time country hymn singing in the church where I grew up. I also improvise inspired by the simple, somewhat elusive, beauty of my home state, Louisiana.

Classic Country-Church style:

For most of history, the only musical instrument a small independent church could expect to have access to was the human voice. Because of this, a very unique style was developed. Four part Gospel consists of four very unique harmonic parts usually all active through the verses and sometimes organized as two and two or one and three part repeats through the chorus. When the churches begain to get pianos, rather than just simply duplicate the vocals, piano players began to use octaves and chords in the bass clef and play the melody and fills for the treble clef. This style, combined with the stunningly optimistic tone of the music makes a style like no other.

The Creeks, Hills, Fields, Trees, and Ponds of Louisiana:

It is said that you should find inspiration from your surroundings, and I suppose I could not ask for better. If you've never been here, no it isn't just one big swamp with New Orleans in it. It is a fascinating portion of the country that hasn't yet been totally converted to a metropolis or a tourist trap. It is filled with beautiful scenery and wildlife. Most of my photography is taken from here.
